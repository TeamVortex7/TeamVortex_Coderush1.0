Chemistry Concepts

Acid-Base Titration

Acid-base titration is a technique used to determine the concentration of an acid or base by neutralizing it with a solution of known concentration. The equivalence point is when the moles of acid equal the moles of base. Indicators like phenolphthalein are used to visually detect the endpoint.

Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Neutralization Reactions

Neutralization reactions occur when an acid and a base react to form salt and water. The pH of the resulting solution depends on the strengths of the acid and base.

  • Strong acid + strong base → Neutral salt (pH ≈ 7)
  • Strong acid + weak base → Acidic salt (pH < 7)
  • Weak acid + strong base → Basic salt (pH > 7)

Precipitation Reactions

Precipitation reactions occur when two soluble salts react to form an insoluble salt (precipitate). These reactions are used in qualitative analysis and industrial processes.

Example: AgNO₃ + NaCl → AgCl (s) + NaNO₃
