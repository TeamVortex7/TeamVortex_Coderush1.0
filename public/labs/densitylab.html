Key Concepts

Archimedes' Principle

Any object immersed in a fluid experiences an upward buoyant force equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the object.

Buoyancy

The ability of a fluid to exert an upward force on an object placed in it, allowing objects with lower density than the fluid to float.

Density

Mass per unit volume. Objects with density lower than the fluid will float, while those with higher density will sink.

Fluid Displacement

When an object is placed in a fluid, it displaces a volume of fluid equal to its own volume, which determines the buoyant force.
